# Climate-Livelihood Shocks and Household Protection Gaps in Somalia

This repository shares concise, derived, non-identifiable outputs from a SIHBS 2022-based analysis of climate-livelihood shocks, erosive coping, household food insecurity, and protection gaps in Somalia.

## Project title

**Climate-Livelihood Shocks and Household Protection Gaps in Somalia: Evidence from SIHBS 2022**

## Purpose

The repository is designed as a compact public research mini-project to communicate selected derived results, figures, and reproducible code templates without sharing restricted microdata.

## Key findings

- Climate-livelihood shocks affected **70.8%** of households.
- Moderate/severe food insecurity affected **42.8%** of households.
- Erosive coping was reported by **18.3%** of households.
- Formal support reached only **4.1%** of households.
- A household protection gap affected **17.1%** of households.
- A severe protection gap affected **9.0%** of households.
- Nomadic and livestock-owning households carried the highest descriptive burdens.
- Drought-related livestock loss was a consistent marker of protection gaps among shock-exposed households.

## Data privacy

Raw SIHBS 2022 microdata are **not included** in this repository. The microdata are managed by the Somalia National Bureau of Statistics and are subject to official data-access and data-use conditions.

This repository includes only:

- aggregated, non-identifiable outputs;
- publication-style figures;
- reproducible code templates;
- public project documentation.
