# Project Brief

## Title

Climate-Livelihood Shocks and Household Protection Gaps in Somalia: Evidence from SIHBS 2022

## Background

Somalia faces recurrent food insecurity shaped by overlapping climate, livelihood, market, conflict, and displacement shocks. This mini-project presents selected derived outputs from a SIHBS 2022-based analysis of household food insecurity, climate-livelihood shock exposure, erosive coping, formal support, and protection gaps.

## Objective

To communicate how household survey evidence can identify shock-exposed households that rely on erosive coping in the absence of formal support.

## Main contribution

The project reframes household food insecurity as a shock-management and protection-gap issue, not only as a poverty issue.

## Policy relevance

The findings support shock-responsive food-security programming, adaptive social protection targeting, and household-level evidence for climate-resilient food-system policy.

## Data note

No raw SIHBS microdata are shared. Only aggregated, non-identifiable outputs and code templates are included.
